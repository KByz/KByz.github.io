What is HEMA?

Historical European Martial Arts (HEMA) is a diverse sport made based on historical melle combat modules from accross Europe. The most common modules found in HEMA clubs are Meyer's longsaword, Rowarth sabre, and 1.33 sword and buckler (from the oldest found manuscript in Europe to date). Sparring is usally between two opponants and uses either synthetic or metal swords. Overall, HEMA is a sport, not a war-game. There are standards of chivlry and safety that we aim to meet in every session. We train to defend and attack with presicion and accuracy, not to simply wave a sword about. If this sounds like your type of sport, then fill in your details in the sign up page and come take part in active history!

Who are we?

HEMA Nelson was founded by Stewart Knapman in 2018. Begining on the fields of Tahunanui, the club quickly grew enough to be hosted in the YMCA hall in ToiToi. Today, the club teaches 5 different modules that covers both the wider realm of HEMA, as well as the world of hsitorical reenactment.
