Who are we?

Founded in 2017, HEMA Nelson began as a small group of enthusiets meeting up at the beach to study the art of historical melee combat. We have since grown into a sizable group who meet up to train every week at the YMCA hall in Toi Toi, Nelson. With a plethora of different modules and a partnership with the local reenactment club, we offer year-round training to both seasoned practitioners and newcomers.

What is HEMA?

Historical European Martial Arts (HEMA) is a diverse sport made based on historical melle combat modules from accross Europe. The most common modules found in HEMA clubs are Meyer's longsword, Rowarth sabre, and 1.33 sword and buckler (from the oldest found manuscript in Europe to date). Sparring is usally between two opponants and uses either synthetic or metal swords. Overall, HEMA is a sport, not a war-game. There are standards of chivlry and safety that we aim to meet in every session. We train to defend and attack with presicion and accuracy, not to simply wave a sword about. If this sounds like your type of sport, then fill in your details in the sign up page and come take part in active history!.

What is Reenactment?

Reenactment is more like living history. It focuses on weaponry, armour, and battle tactics from history and aims to recreate the experience of a battle. Reenactment is a more immersive experience than HEMA, as it involves larger groups and teamwork during sparing. Reenactment is a great way to learn more about history and part-take in recreated battles and events that are held throughout the country such as the battle of Clontarf or the battle of Hastings.
